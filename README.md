

# Alpha Research Agent

**Alpha Research Agent** is a multi-agent investment research system built using Microsoft Foundry as part of the **Microsoft AI Agents League Hackathon (Reasoning Agents Track)**.

The platform simulates an institutional investment committee by combining multiple analyst perspectives—including macroeconomic, fundamental, risk, and market sentiment analysis—before generating a final committee recommendation and investment memo.

## Overview

Traditional investment research requires analysts to evaluate opportunities from multiple perspectives before making a recommendation. Alpha Research Agent recreates this workflow using AI agents that specialize in different areas of financial analysis. Rather than relying on a single AI response, Alpha Research Agent simulates an institutional investment committee. Specialized analysts evaluate the company from different perspectives before a Committee Chair synthesizes the findings and issues a final recommendation.

Given a company name or stock ticker, the system:

* Researches the company and industry
* Evaluates financial performance and business fundamentals
* Assesses risks and market conditions
* Analyzes recent developments and sentiment
* Synthesizes findings through a committee-style decision process
* Produces a professional investment research memo

## Features

### Macro Analyst

Evaluates macroeconomic conditions, industry trends, interest rates, and broader market factors affecting the company.

### Fundamental Analyst

Reviews business model, competitive advantages, financial performance, growth drivers, and valuation considerations.

### Risk Analyst

Identifies key risks, concentration concerns, regulatory issues, geopolitical exposure, and downside scenarios.

### Market Sentiment Analyst

Assesses analyst sentiment, recent news, investor positioning, and market perception.

### Committee Chair

Aggregates analyst findings, resolves disagreements, conducts committee voting, and issues the final recommendation.

## Architecture

The system uses structured multi-step reasoning to simulate how institutional investment teams evaluate opportunities. Each analyst contributes an independent perspective before the Committee Chair synthesizes findings into a final recommendation.

This design improves transparency, encourages balanced analysis, and provides a more realistic research workflow than a single-agent approach.
